Ashlyn Stewart

Doctoral student in nineteenth-century American Literature and DH at UNL


I am a third-year doctoral student at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln studying nineteenth-century American literature and digital humanities. My research interests center on national periodicals from the mid-nineteenth century, particularly Harper publications. I also work on two digital archives: the Walt Whitman Archive and the Charles W. Chesnutt Archive. I hold a BA in English and History from the University of Denver and an MA in English from UNL.


Work Samples:

Digital Archives

As an Assistant Editor at the Walt Whitman Archive and Charles W. Chesnutt Archive, I have contributed to several NEH- and NHPRC-funded projects. Informed by digital scholarly editing theory, I encode a wide variety of texts to be available for open access on the web.
