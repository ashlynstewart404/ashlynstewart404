About me

I am a first-year doctoral student at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln studying nineteenth-century American literature and digital humanities.  My research interests center on national periodicals from the mid-nineteenth century, particularly Harper publications.  I also work on two digital archives: the Walt Whitman Archive and the Charles W. Chesnutt Archive.  I hold a BA in English and History from the University of Denver and an MA in English from UNL.  

Digital Archives

As an Assistant Editor at the Walt Whitman Archive and Charles W. Chesnutt Archive, I have contributed to several NEH- and NHPRC-funded projects. Informed by digital scholarly editing theory, I encode a wide variety of texts to be available for open access on the web.

Writing and Research

I have published my own research on Harper's Weekly, a collaborative research guide for nineteenth-century American magazines, and a (forthcoming) collaborative book chapter about Walt Whitman's views of his personal archive.
